Private Trust Services

A trust is a legal arrangement whereby one party (the custodian) transfers its assets and its legal title to another party (the trustee) to hold and manage them for the benefit of the custodian himself and/or other parties (the beneficiaries). As a licensed and insured trust company, FGA Trust brings a wealth of experience in managing trusts and a contractual obligation to put the best interests of the trust beneficiaries first. In accordance with the trustee’s instructions and objectives, this obligation includes managing the trust to protect and grow its assets and to use those assets solely for the benefit of the beneficiaries.

 

Simply stated, a trust can be classified according to two major attributes: (1) whether it is revocable; and (2) whether it was created while the trustee was alive. A trustee can change a revocable trust, but cannot change an irrevocable trust. Therefore, a living trust is created by the custodian while he or she is alive. Otherwise, it is a willed trust, created by the provisions of the principal’s will, and exists only after the principal’s death.
